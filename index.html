SeConRob: Self-Configuring Multi-Step
Robotic Workflows for Non-Destructive Testing

PROFACTOR GmbH · Fraunhofer IZFP · ACS Solutions GmbH · OTTO FUCHS · Marposs · Safe Metal · École Centrale de Lyon

EU Horizon Europe · Grant Agreement No. 101118511

    Introduction

    In modern manufacturing of safety-critical metallic components, production chains often consist of multiple dependent steps: a part is first inspected, then repaired or reworked based on the findings, and finally re-inspected to verify the repair. Today, these multi-step workflows are rarely fully automated because each downstream action must be individually programmed by an expert who interprets the inspection results and adapts the robot program accordingly.

    The SeConRob project — Self-Configuring Multi-Step Robotic Workflows — is a European research initiative that aims to close this gap. Funded by the EU Horizon Europe programme, SeConRob develops methods that enable robots to automatically self-configure for each step of a production chain based on the outcomes of the previous step. Inspection data is no longer just a pass/fail verdict: it becomes the direct input that generates the robot program for the next process, whether that is grinding, welding, gouging, or polishing.

    Motivation & Challenge

    Safety-critical metallic parts in the automotive and aerospace industries — such as cast engine housings, forged turbine discs, and structural weldments — require rigorous non-destructive testing (NDT) followed by targeted rework of any detected anomalies. Current practice demands highly skilled NDT engineers who manually interpret inspection results and programme each subsequent robot action, making these workflows slow, expensive and difficult to scale.

    The core challenge is variability: every part may carry a defect at a different location, of a different size, or of a different type. A robot system that can handle this variability autonomously — without stopping production for manual reprogramming — would deliver substantial gains in productivity, resource efficiency, and quality assurance.

    Additionally, state-of-the-art NDT systems produce far richer data than a simple pass/fail flag. Phased-array ultrasonic testing (PAUT), magneto-optical imaging, and eddy-current scanning provide full volumetric defect maps with size, orientation, and depth information. SeConRob exploits this information to configure rework robots automatically and precisely.

    Technical Approach

    SeConRob combines three technological pillars to achieve autonomous self-configuration:

    1 · Robot-Based NDT with Phased-Array Ultrasonics

    ACS Solutions GmbH integrates its high-technology phased-array ultrasonic testing (PAUT) systems on industrial robot arms. Electronic beam steering and focusing provide high-resolution volumetric defect maps of complex-shaped parts — including curved surfaces and near-net-shape forgings — that conventional fixed-mount probes cannot handle efficiently. Magneto-optical inspection developed by Fraunhofer IZFP complements PAUT for surface and near-surface crack detection in ferromagnetic materials.

    2 · AI-Driven Data Analysis & Defect Characterisation

    Fraunhofer IZFP develops deep-learning models that process raw inspection signals and produce structured defect descriptions: location (3-D coordinates), extent, orientation and severity class. These outputs feed directly into a parametric process planning module that selects the appropriate rework strategy (e.g., gouging depth, weld bead geometry, grinding path) and generates a robot programme automatically — with no human intervention.

    3 · Reinforcement Learning for Long-Term Optimisation

    A reinforcement-learning (RL) feedback loop collects outcome data from every completed rework cycle — post-repair inspection results, surface finish measurements, cycle times — and uses this to continuously refine the process models. Over time the system adapts to material and geometry variations encountered in real production, reducing both scrap rates and the number of re-inspection iterations required.

    Industrial Use Cases

    The SeConRob technologies are validated on two complementary industrial demonstrations representing different sectors and process types.

    Use Case 1 · Safety-Critical Cast & Forged Parts (Safe Metal)

    Safe Metal (France) operates a production line for safety-critical automotive cast and forged components. The SeConRob demonstrator at Safe Metal integrates a complete control-and-repair loop:

    1. Robotic magneto-optical and visual inspection to detect surface defects.
    2. Automated defect characterisation and process planning.
    3. Robotic gouging to remove the defect material.
    4. Robotic welding to fill the gouged cavity.
    5. Robotic grinding and polishing to restore surface geometry.
    6. Final robotic re-inspection to confirm conformance.

    Use Case 2 · Aerospace Forged Components (OTTO FUCHS)

    OTTO FUCHS (Germany) produces high-strength aluminium and titanium forgings for aerospace applications. The SeConRob demonstrator at OTTO FUCHS focuses on robot-based phased-array ultrasonic inspection of near-net-shape forgings followed by adaptive finishing (grinding and polishing) driven directly by the inspection findings. The complex curved geometries and tight tolerances of aerospace forgings make this an especially demanding test of the system's self-configuration capability.

    NDT System Performance

    The robotic PAUT system developed by ACS Solutions was evaluated on reference test blocks and production-representative forgings. The key performance indicators achieved so far are:

    • Detection of flat-bottom holes (FBH) down to ø 1 mm at depths up to 80 mm in aluminium forgings.
    • Full volumetric coverage of complex curved surfaces with an automated path planning algorithm that adapts the scanning trajectory to the CAD model of the part.
    • Defect localisation accuracy better than ± 1 mm in 3D, enabling precise targeting of downstream rework operations.

    AI Analysis & Process Planning

    The AI-based defect characterisation pipeline (Fraunhofer IZFP) was trained and validated on a library of annotated PAUT and magneto-optical scans. On the validation set, it achieves:

    • Defect type classification accuracy > 95 % across the four relevant defect classes (porosity, inclusions, cracks, shrinkage cavities).
    • Automated generation of a rework robot programme within < 30 s of completing the inspection scan.
    • Consistent elimination of the manual interpretation step that previously accounted for 40–60 % of total workflow time.

    Consortium

    SeConRob brings together seven complementary organisations from five European countries, covering the full chain from fundamental research to industrial implementation.

    PROFACTOR GmbH Austria

    Project coordinator. Robot process planning and automation for non-ultrasonic inspection and rework steps.

    Fraunhofer IZFP Germany

    AI-driven data analysis, magneto-optical inspection, defect characterisation and automated process parameter generation.

    ACS Solutions GmbH Germany

    Robot-based phased-array ultrasonic testing systems for volumetric inspection of complex metallic parts.

    OTTO FUCHS KG Germany

    Aerospace use case: robotic inspection and adaptive finishing of high-strength aluminium and titanium forgings.

    Marposs S.p.A. Italy

    Industrial inspection and precision measurement equipment; integration of in-process gauging into the robotic workflow.

    Safe Metal France

    Automotive use case: complete robotic inspect–gouge–weld–grind loop for safety-critical cast and forged components.

    École Centrale de Lyon / ENISE France

    Academic partner. Physical process models for grinding, welding and polishing; reinforcement-learning framework.
